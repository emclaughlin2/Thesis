We also compare our choice of using feeddown corrected particle spectra from PHENIX to the particle spectra from STAR also for \sqsntwo \auau. Due to the PHENIX data having feeddown corrections applied and the STAR not having feeddown corrections applied, pion invariant yields were selected for comparison. These yields should still be affected by the feeddown corrections, but less so than protons or Kaons. Since the STAR data does not include feeddown corrections we expect this measurement of invariant yield of pions to be slightly higher in the lower $p_{T}$ range than the pion yield from PHENIX. A comparison of the STAR and PHENIX pion particle spectra from \cite{star_id_particle_spectra} and \cite{PhysRevC.93.024901} are included in Fig. \ref{fig:star_vs_phenix_pion_spectra} for three separate centrality ranges used in our particle reweighting methodology. The spectra have slight disagreement in the low $p_{T}$ range as expected from the absence versus presence of feeddown corrections, with the STAR measurement yield being slightly higher than PHENIX as expected; otherwise the spectra agree.
